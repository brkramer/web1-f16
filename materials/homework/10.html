After several weeks of web design you should have an understanding of the steps required to design and build a website. From now until the end of the semester your task is to bring all of your projects to completion. Your focus should be building your Personal Portfolio website and completing your Client Site.

Deliverables

Due by the start of next class.

  1. Client site. If you haven't already, create a new folder to hold your final client website. Link to this from your student page. You should have html, CSS, images and content.
  2. Personal portfolio. If you haven't already, create a new folder to hold your final personal portfolio website. Link to this from your student page. You should have html and start to build CSS, images and content.

Assessing Completion

For each of your projects, reviewing each of the following concepts will help you decided what work still needs to be done. Due by the start of our last class.
  1. Semantic content. Your site content is present and codified in HTML.
  2. CSS styling and layout. You have styled and laid out your website with CSS.
  3. Imagery. You properly formatted images for the web and they are present on your website.
  4. Validated. You have run a validation tool on your website and there are no major issues.
  5. Visual design. Your site has a compelling and readable visual aesthetic.
  6. Usability. Your site fulfills various user needs and does so in a learnable consistent manner.
  7. Device Testing. Your site is usable on a variety of platforms, screen sizes, browsers and screen readers.
  8. Font zoom. Your site does not break for reasonable levels of font zoom (shrinking and growing).
  9. Responsive. If appropriate your website varies according to screen size and is usable at all interim screen sizes.
  10. JavaScript. Optional. You have included any JavaScript libraries to improve the usability or excitement of your website.
